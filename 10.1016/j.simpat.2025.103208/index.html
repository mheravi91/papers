Physics‑informed data‑driven modeling of rock motion dynamics in excavation

We blend physics-grounded priors with data-driven inference to model rock motion during excavation. A digital twin simulator supplies high-fidelity trajectories that inform training, yielding robust predictions under changing soil conditions.

  • AuthorsMohammad Heravi · Amirmasoud Molaei · Reza Ghabcheloo

Abstract

The excavation process produces complex rock motion dynamics that standard empirical models struggle to capture. We introduce a hybrid framework that embeds Newtonian priors into a data-driven surrogate trained on traces from a high-fidelity simulator. The method improves stability in extrapolative settings and reduces error bounds under sparse sensing.

Highlights

  • Physics-informed neural surrogates track 6-DoF rock motion across excavation cycles.
  • Simulation-derived datasets accelerate training while safeguarding real-world operations.
  • Validated against baseline empirical models with substantial gains in prediction fidelity.
